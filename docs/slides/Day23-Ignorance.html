class: center, middle # Engineered ignorance: Knowledge that gets deliberately hidden from the public

Big Tobacco Hides Cancer Risks

  • Early 1950s, considerable expansion of research on tobacco health risks (e.g. lung cancer)
  • Tobacco industry seeks to produce knowledge that will undo effects of emerging research:
    • Enlisted public relations firm Hill & Knowlton
    • Determined that advertising won’t work because self-interested by definition
    • Sought out credentialed medical skeptics to engineer controversy
    • Created Tobacco Industry Research Committee and funded research to sew doubt/uncertainty

Medical Funding

  • Nature reports that much medical funding goes to the “big three” - HIV/AIDS, malaria and tuberculosis
  • Funding diverted away from “neglected tropical diseases”
    • Impact billions of people and disproportionately impact most vulnerable populations
    • Examples: dengue, leprosy, trachoma
  • Covid-19 further diverted funding from these programs

Environmental Testing following Hurricane Katrina

  • Only test contaminants designated by law (<200 of 8200 in TSCA) on public properties
  • Tend to test where there was flooding, not where there may be the most contaminants
    • Most serious flooding escaped “sliver along the river” - location of the city’s industrial corridor
    • Untested areas include several brownfields and areas with history of manufacturing expansion

Colony Collapse Disease

  • US beekeepers have lost almost 1/3 of bees every year since 2006
  • Scientific studies of insecticides on bees were designed to test the individual lethal effects of a chemical on a bee population
    • Part of a “control-oriented” scientific culture
  • Failed to account for slow, cumulative effects over time and the interactions of chemicals and pathogens

Kleinman, Daniel Lee, and Sainath Suryanarayanan. 2013. “Dying Bees and the Social Production of Ignorance.” Science, Technology & Human Values 38 (4): 492–517. https://doi.org/10.1177/0162243912442575.
